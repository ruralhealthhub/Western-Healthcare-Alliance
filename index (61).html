The Need in Rural Colorado

Rural Colorado needs specialty care that’s coordinated — not fragmented.

Across rural Colorado, specialist shortages and long drives push care out of reach — and when in-person care is truly needed, the connection to a regional hospital is too often left to chance. The gaps span a broad range of specialties, from behavioral health and addiction medicine to diabetes and beyond. A coordinated network changes that: care is managed locally, and coordinated referrals to regional hospitals are simple when they’re required.

Distance & workforce gaps

In 2023, all but two Colorado counties were designated mental-health provider shortage areas, and many rural counties have zero psychiatrists. Long travel means patients defer care until an intervention is needed.

Fragmented hand-offs

Without a coordinated path, referrals stall and follow-ups slip. With 64% of rural & frontier counties lacking hospital obstetric services, patients fall through the gaps between local care and the specialist they need.

Avoidable readmissions

Patients discharged without coordinated local follow-up come back. Returning them to the same managing tele-specialist closes the loop and cuts 30-day readmissions.

Patient Health

Healthier patients, cared for closer to home.

The clearest beneficiary of a coordinated network is the patient. When specialty care is managed locally — and connected upward only when in-person treatment is truly needed — rural patients are seen sooner, treated closer to home, and spared the disruptions that come with fragmented care, from long drives to avoidable trips to the emergency room.

Care that stays close to home

Local access to specialty care means patients are managed in their own community, in a relationship they trust — without long drives for every consult and follow-up. Care that’s easier to reach is care patients are more likely to keep up with.

Fewer trips to the ER

When patients can reach a specialist early — or a local provider can get a quick curbside answer — problems are caught and managed before they escalate. That heads off avoidable emergency visits that are stressful, disruptive, and often preventable with timely access.

Fewer unnecessary hospital stays

Conditions caught and managed earlier are less likely to require an admission that better access could have prevented. And when a patient is discharged, returning them to the same local team for coordinated follow-up helps keep them from coming back.

Partnership

Extend coordinated care without adding infrastructure or staff.

The program brings coordinated specialty care into each rural hospital — strengthening connected care and reducing readmissions — without any hospital recruiting or hiring additional providers. The Western Healthcare Alliance serves as the distribution arm, so every member hospital can offer the same program to its community.

Value for the rural hospital

Care that stays at your hospital.

Patients stay under active management by an RHH tele-specialist at your hospital. When in-person care is truly needed, it’s a clean specialist-to-specialist referral — only the cases that genuinely need it are sent on, and the patient returns to your local team afterward, with continuity intact on both sides.
